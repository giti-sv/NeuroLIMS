# Brain Regions

## 1. Frontal Lobe
**Main Function:** Decision-making, planning, problem-solving, voluntary movement  
**Why included in NeuroLIMS:** Helps users learn executive function and motor control

## 2. Parietal Lobe
**Main Function:** Sensory processing, spatial awareness  
**Why included in NeuroLIMS:** Helps users explore touch and body awareness functions

## 3. Temporal Lobe
**Main Function:** Hearing, memory, language  
**Why included in NeuroLIMS:** Connects well with hippocampus and memory concepts

## 4. Occipital Lobe
**Main Function:** Vision processing  
**Why included in NeuroLIMS:** Easy for users to understand the visual function

## 5. Cerebellum
**Main Function:** Balance and coordination  
**Why included in NeuroLIMS:** Useful for simple movement-related simulation outcomes

## 6. Hippocampus
**Main Function:** Memory formation and learning  
**Why included in NeuroLIMS:** Important for memory-focused experiments

## 7. Amygdala
**Main Function:** Emotion and fear processing  
**Why included in NeuroLIMS:** Good for emotion-related learning
